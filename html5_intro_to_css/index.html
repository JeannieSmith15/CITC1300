"Symbol of Freedom and Hope"

The Statue of Liberty, standing proudly in New York Harbor, is one of the most recognized symbols of freedom and democracy. A gift from France in 1886, Lady Liberty represents the enduring friendship between the two nations and welcomes millions of visitors each year.

"A Monument of Freedom"

Designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and built with the help of Gustave Eiffel, the Statue of Liberty was dedicated on October 28, 1886. Standing at 305 feet (93 meters) from base to torch, it has been a symbol of hope for immigrants arriving in America.

Interesting Facts:

  • The statue is made of copper, which has turned green due to oxidation.
  • The tablet in Lady Liberty’s hand is inscribed with the date July 4, 1776 (America’s Independence Day).
  • There are 354 steps to the crown, offering breathtaking views of New York City.

Plan Your Visit

Visitors can explore the Statue of Liberty Museum, take in panoramic views from the pedestal, and even visit nearby Ellis Island, where millions of immigrants entered the U.S.
